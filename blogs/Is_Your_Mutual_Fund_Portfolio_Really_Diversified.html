Is Your Mutual Fund Portfolio really Diversified?

“Don’t put all your eggs in a single basket” is one sentence that every investor has heard about and tries to reduce systematic risk. i.e. One hopes the loss from a single company (say due to issues with the management) will be potentially offset by gains in other companies in the portfolio. However, what if all the companies in a fund belong to the same sector. One is exposed to systematic risks . i.e any macroeconomic factors will affect all the companies in a sector and the loss could be substantial.

So the easiest way out is to invest in multiple mutual funds across different categories (Large cap / Active funds / flexicaps etc). But doing so without paying attention can lead to a portfolio which is not as diversified as one thinks it is.

Let us take an example of a portfolio where one has invested in Axis Bluechip fund and Axis Flexi Cap Fund.

The result is shocking. Although there are only 36 stocks which are common and 28 stocks which are not common, when the holding percentage of those stocks are taken into account, 95% of Axis Flexi Cap Fund simply replicates the returns from Axis Bluechip Fund. This means, there is absolutely no diversification in investing in these two funds together.

Although, everyone wants to diversify their portfolios, it is not easy to check if it is diversified enough because considerable time and math is required to figure out the exact allocation of funds to individual stocks / sectors in a mutual fund. It is all the more challenging to find the same for multiple funds in a portfolio.

If there are multiple mutual funds in your portfolio, it is important to check for and eliminate such overlaps.

What can be done to avoid under diversification in your Mutual Fund Portfolio?

  1. Check sectoral allocation

    Investment in just Index fund could have more exposure to Banks and Software sectors. If you would like to capture up cycles in some sectors like Auto for example, You could allocate certain amount the the same and check how your new portfolio looks like.

    The portfolio becomes less skewed towards certain industry after adding a consumer and Pharma fund.

  1. Check for overlaps across all pairwise combinations funds in your portfolio

    You can see that in a portfolio consisting 4 mutual funds, there is 60% overlap between UTI Nifty 50 Index fund and Axis Multicap Fund. So investing in these two together, is not diversifying the portfolio enough.

  1. Avoid investing in multiple funds from a same fund manager The investment strategy of a fund manager / AMC originates from the research and interpretation of facts. There will be inherent biases in the process and there is a high likelihood of blind-spots, i.e. at an AMC level, they could overlook risks in particular sector. Therefore, one must diversify at the AMC level as well.
